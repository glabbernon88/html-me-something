Travels to Italy 2009

Italy 2009

A Tuscan Adventure

In July of 2009, I took a memorable trip to Italy with my brother and his wife. We stayed in Lucca, a town in the Tuscan region, famous for its intact Renaissance‑era city walls. The walls were originally built as protection from military invaders, but as they lost their military importance, the top of the walls became a pedestrian promenade.

We stayed at "Villa Lavanda", an 1800's era restored Italian villa, nestled in the hills outside of Lucca.

We took several day trips, the first to Pisa to visit the Piazza del Duomo, home of the Leaning Tower of Pisa.

We followed that by a day trip to Florence. While there, we toured The Uffizi and Galleria dell’ Accademia, museums home to famous works by Michaelangelo, Boticelli and many others, and did a walking tour of the city, including a stroll on the Ponte Vecchio.

The highlight of the trip was a tour of the Ferrari factory in Maranello. We toured the museum, research and development (though they were fairly secretive in that area), viewed the production lines from start to finish: assembly lines, upholstery production, final painting, and watching them test drive the finished product (how do I apply for that job??!). Definitely a once in a lifetime tour for me.

We capped off the trip with a visit to a small local winery, having lunch overlooking the vineyards when we were done. It was the perfect ending to the trip, and made me eager to go back again soon.
